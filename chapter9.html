️ Weighted Graphs

Definition: Graphs where edges have costs/weights

Examples:

  • Road networks (distances)
  • Flight routes (costs)
  • Network latency (time)

Dijkstra's Algorithm

Purpose: Find shortest path from source to all nodes

Created by: Edsger Dijkstra (1956)

1. Initialize distances to ∞ (source=0) 2. Pick unvisited node with min distance 3. For each neighbor: relax edge 4. Mark current as visited 5. Repeat until all visited

Complexity: O(V²) or O((V+E)log V) with min-heap

Heuristics

Definition: Rule of thumb for fast approximate solutions

Trade-off:

  • Speed vs Accuracy
  • Good enough vs Perfect

Key Takeaways

  • Weighted graphs have costs on edges
  • Dijkstra finds shortest paths
  • Uses greedy approach
  • Only works with non-negative weights
  • Priority queue optimizes

Heuristics in graph problems

Shortest Queue Heuristic:

"Pick the Line That Looks Fastest"

  • Real-life: Network routing path least traffic, picking shortest line at market
  • Problem: Doesn't account for different processing speeds of each "lane"

Frequency-Based Heuristic:

"Popular Routes Are Usually Good"

  • Real-life: Social media "People you may know", following most-used hiking trail
  • Problem: Popular doesn't always mean optimal for your specific situation

Avoid Congestion Heuristic:

"Stay Away from Busy Areas"

  • Real-life: Taking back roads to avoid highway traffic, shopping during off-peak hours
  • Problem: "Busy" areas might be faster or better despite the crowds

Problems with simple heuristics

The Local Optimum Problem:

  • Hill climbing: Heuristic finds a "pretty good" solution but misses the "best" solution
  • No backtracking: Once you make a choice, you can't undo it
  • Tunnel vision: Focusing on immediate gains instead of long-term optimal path

The Information Problem:

  • Incomplete data: Heuristics work with limited information
  • Changing conditions: Traffic, weather, or network conditions change constantly
  • Context missing: A route might be short but dangerous, crowded, or expensive
